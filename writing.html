Writing Resources

Writing Papers

In U2 and U3, more and more evaluations become term papers and oral presentations, rather than multiple choice examinations. McGill has many resources available to assist you in writing your first papers in Physiology.

The McGill Writing Centre

The McGill Writing Centre offers tutorial services, where you can walk in with your work-in-progress and receive guidance on how to achieve clarity, flow, and voice in your writing. This service is available by appointment, but also by drop-in during scheduled drop-in hours.

This is NOT a service for proofreading and editing, rather one to improve coherency and structure.

For more information, please visit their website.

Additionally, the McGill Writing Centre offers CEAP 250: Research Essay and Rhetoric, a course geared towards teaching science students how to write papers efficiently and effectively. This course is offered both semesters and can count towards elective credits in your B.Sc degree. Click here to see the syllabus.

Guide to Research and Using EndNote

The McGill Library holds periodic workshops to assist you in conducting research online, showing you reputable primary sources (PubMed, GoogleScholar, Web of Science, Scopus, etc.) that your McGill login gives you FREE access to, as well as how to request texts in print. These workshops also include referencing using EndNote, a helpful citation management system. In fact, our wonderful McGill librarians have compiled all this information into a handy document for you.

For more information, please contact a McGill librarian.

Physiology Librarian

Don't know where to start? Every McGill department has a liaison librarian to assist you in your field of research. Our physiology librarian has compiled a Physiology research guide filled with research tips and physiology databases for you to browse, providing you a good place to start your research.
